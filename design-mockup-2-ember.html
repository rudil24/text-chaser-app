There's a particular quality to the hour before dawn — not silence exactly, but a suspension. The city held its breath and Marcus held his too, watching the last ember of a cigarette trace its descent from the third-floor window across the alley. He had been standing in the same spot for eleven minutes. He knew because he had counted each one.

The notebook in his coat pocket had twelve pages left. He had promised himself the story would end before those pages did, that some version of resolution would arrive before the paper ran out — but promises made to oneself at 4 a.m. carry the weight of smoke. He pulled the collar up. The alley smelled of rain that hadn't come yet, the electric pressure of a sky deciding whether to break.

Design Specification
Proposed App Name
PYRE
"Keep writing or burn."
"A pressure writing engine."
PYRE is a funeral fire — historically, it is the last act of destruction before transformation. It is short, hard, one syllable, all-caps natural. It carries the full weight of the concept: your writing exists as long as the fire lives. Stop feeding it and it consumes itself. The word is ancient and visceral with zero startup/app baggage. It beats IGNITE (verb, diffuse), EMBER (passive), BLAZE (too casual). It's a noun. It's a ceremony. It's a threat.

UI Behavior Notes
Timer ring: SVG stroke-dashoffset animation drains clockwise over 5s. Resets on each keystroke. At under 3s, transitions to danger palette (red-orange). Under 1s: full red, ring vibrates via transform animation.
Editor heat creep: At under 3s, radial-gradient overlays grow from window edges — bottom corners first, spreading inward. The text area literally appears to heat up.
Danger pulse: Timer digits scale slightly (1.0 → 1.04) and shift from orange to yellow-white at peak brightness, simulating heat emission.
Deletion flash: On zero — full-screen flash to near-white (overexposed), then immediate cut to deletion state. Text dissolves via opacity + blur + color shift to burnt orange-red before fading out.
Scanlines: Deletion state uses repeating-gradient scanlines with a high-frequency flicker (0.08s step-end) — simulates a screen burning out.
Wordmark glow: drop-shadow filter animates on a 3s ease-in-out loop. Appears to breathe like a coal that won't quite die.
